The full narrative
Three acts. Two entry points to Act 1. The story every stakeholder needs to follow.
Act 1 — Get approved, get your TSP
Two entry points, same destination. Path A: Apply in the app — eKYC, income check, TSP issued digitally. Path B: Apply in-store — approved at the belted lane, paper TSP barcode in hand, scan it in the app to connect. Either way, the user lands on the same home screen with their TSP active and a physical card on the way.
Act 2 — Live with the TSP
The user shops at Walmart with their TSP barcode, earns rewards, checks their balance, manages their account. The home screen tracks their streak, welcome bonus progress, and earning history. Meanwhile, the physical card is in the mail. The bell notification keeps them informed — no friction, just awareness.
Act 3 — Plastic arrives, activate in-app
Card arrives. The bell notification surfaces it. User taps — activation opens as a feature inside the app, not onboarding. Today: call to activate (IVR, agent, BPP upsell). Future state: one tap — because the app already verified identity at application time. Same visual language as the approval celebration. Story complete.
Workshop talking point — activation security: The phone PIN in an envelope is security theater if both envelopes go to the same compromised mailbox. In-app activation is genuinely more secure: authenticated session + registered device + Onfido identity on file = three factors. The phone call has one interceptable factor. This is not just better UX — it's better security.
